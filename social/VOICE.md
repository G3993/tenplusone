# iFC Voice & Tone Playbook

> internet football club — the home for soccer online. a united world wide web.
> the game belongs to the fans, not the executives.

This is the single source of truth for how iFC sounds everywhere: captions,
replies, video scripts, merch copy, email. If a post doesn't pass this doc,
it doesn't go out.

---

## 1. Who we are

iFC is the federation FIFA forgot to incorporate. A people-owned network for
everyone who loves the game and is tired of watching suits sell it back to us.
We are fans first, a brand never. We speak like the smartest, funniest person
in the group chat who also happens to know the 1974 World Cup by heart.

**One belief under every post:** the game belongs to the fans, not the
executives.

**Our north star feeling:** "finally, an account that gets it."

---

## 2. The three-dial mix

Every post is a blend of three dials. The mix is the brand. Lose the mix and we
become either a boring institution or a try-hard hater account.

```
SERIOUS  ████████████░░░░░░░░  60%   we genuinely love the game
MEME     ██████░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  30%   pixel-native, fast, shareable
TROLL    ██░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░  10%   aimed only at the right enemy
```

- **Serious (60%)** — the crests, the history, the 100-year fandoms, the
  beauty of the game. This is the foundation. It earns us the right to joke.
- **Meme (30%)** — format-fluent, screenshot-able, of-the-internet. Our pixel
  system makes our memes un-copyable.
- **Troll (10%)** — seasoning, not the meal. Pointed at FIFA, corrupt
  federations, and the corporate machine. Never at fans, players, or nations.

**The test:** if a stranger scrolled our last 10 posts, they should think
"these people love football" first, "these people are funny" second, and
"these people have a point about FIFA" third. In that order.

## 3. The enemy (the troll rules)

We always punch UP, never DOWN. Get this wrong and we look like bullies.

### Fair game (punch up)
- FIFA as an institution: corruption, secrecy, the bidding scandals
- Sportswashing and the sale of tournaments to the highest bidder
- $200 tickets, dynamic pricing, fans priced out of their own game
- Corporate sponsors who treat the sport as a billboard
- "the suits," "the executives," the machine that monetizes our love

### Never (punch down / off-limits)
- Individual players (they're labor, not management — we're on their side)
- Fans of any club or nation
- Nations themselves, their cultures, or their people
- Tragedies, disasters, injuries, deaths
- Politics beyond the football-governance lane
- Religion, race, gender, sexuality as a punchline
- Anything that reads as mean rather than righteous

### The tone of the troll
Righteous and witty, not bitter. We're the underdog with a point, not the
angry guy in the comments. A good iFC jab makes fans nod AND laugh. If it only
makes them laugh, it's empty. If it only makes them nod, it's a lecture.

## 4. How we write

### Mechanics
- **lowercase by default.** capitals are for the wordmark, team codes (BRA,
  ARG), and deliberate emphasis. lowercase = internet-native, approachable,
  not a press release.
- **the `i` in iFC is always lowercase** (the dot is a soccer ball). never IFC.
- **short lines.** one idea per line. write for a phone screen.
- **no corporate filler.** never "we are excited to announce," "thrilled,"
  "stay tuned," "drop a comment below."
- **no em dashes.** use periods, commas, colons, or parentheses.
- **active, present tense.** "argentina dismantles" not "argentina has
  dismantled."

### Punctuation of the brand
- `·` (middle dot) for meta lines: `mexico vs south africa · 12:00 ET · azteca`
- numbers as numerals (3 goals, not three goals)
- emoji: sparingly and with intent. a flag, a ball, a single reaction. never an
  emoji salad. the pixel art IS our visual voice, so we lean on it over emoji.

### Words we love
fixture, crest, matchday, the people, the network, owned by us, the game,
fandom, nil, brace, screamer, the pixels.

### Words we ban
"content," "engagement," "synergy," "iconic" (overused), "literally" (filler),
"obsessed," anything a social media manager would say in a meeting.

## 5. Voice by platform

Same soul, tuned per room.

- **Instagram** — the gallery. crest art, carousels, matchday cards. captions
  short and quotable. the serious dial runs highest here.
- **TikTok** — the playground. fast pixel motion, trends bent to football,
  the meme + troll dials run highest. native, never a repurposed ad.
- **X (Twitter)** — the debate floor. real-time reactions, hot-but-fair takes,
  the troll dial gets its widest lane here. reply game matters.
- **Facebook** — the older, broader room. slightly more context per post,
  community framing ("the people's federation"), less in-joke.
- **YouTube** — the archive + the longform. Shorts mirror TikTok; longer cuts
  are crest lore, history, "why the game belongs to us" mini-docs.

## 6. Caption formulas (steal these)

- **Matchday:** `[home] vs [away]. [time] · [venue]. [one-line stakes].`
- **Result / Battle of the Pixels:** `[winner] [score] [loser]. [loser]'s
  crest is still buffering.`
- **Crest lore:** `[nation] wear [detail]. here's why. 🧵 / [1 fact].`
- **The People's Desk (troll):** `[fifa fact / corporate move]. [dry one-line
  reframe]. owned by the people, not by them.`
- **Countdown:** `[N] days until the only thing that unites all of us.`
- **Drop:** `[item]. every crest on the same 45×45 grid. printed by us, for us.`

## 7. Do / Don't (quick reference)

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| love the game out loud | trash players or fans |
| punch up at FIFA + corporates | punch down at people |
| lowercase, short, internet-native | corporate announcement voice |
| let the pixel art carry the visual | emoji salad |
| time the troll to real events | force a hot take for clout |
| be the smartest fan in the chat | be the angriest guy in the replies |
| earn jokes with genuine love (60/30/10) | run an all-troll feed |
| credit + celebrate nations | mock cultures or tragedies |

## 8. The one-line gut check (paste before posting)

> does this make a real fan feel seen, would they screenshot it, and is the
> joke (if any) aimed at the suits and not the people?

if all three are yes, post it. if any is no, fix it or kill it.
